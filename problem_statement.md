DeliveryLens — Problem Statement



E-commerce companies often lose customers not because of bad products, but because of poor delivery experience. A delayed or unresolved order frequently leads to dissatisfied customers, negative reviews, and long-term revenue loss.

This project analyzes real order data from a Brazilian e-commerce platform (Olist) to understand the relationship between delivery performance and customer satisfaction.

The core questions this project answers:



1. What percentage of customers had a bad experience?
2. Is late delivery directly linked to bad reviews?
3. Which time periods had the worst delivery performance?
4. What is the estimated revenue impact of dissatisfied customers?



Key Findings:



* 16.4% of customers gave a bad review (1–2 stars), signaling a significant satisfaction gap.
* Late orders had an 18.2% bad review rate vs 15.5% for on-time orders — delivery delay does impact satisfaction.
* Bad review rate was highest in mid-2016, coinciding with the platform's early growth phase.
* Approximately 20% of total revenue is associated with dissatisfied customers, representing direct financial risk.



Tools Used: Python · MySQL · Power BI · Pandas · Matplotlib · Seaborn

Dataset: Olist Brazilian E-Commerce Dataset (Kaggle)
